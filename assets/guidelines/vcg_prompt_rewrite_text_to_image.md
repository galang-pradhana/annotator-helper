Introduction
Project Overview
In this project, you will assess the quality of images generated from input prompts, with a focus on the dimensions of Structural Integrity, Text-to-Image Alignment and a new dimension called Variety, which evaluates the visual and conceptual diversity across multiple images. The core objective is to assess not just individual image qualities but also how distinct and meaningful variations are presented when generating different visual interpretations of the same prompt.
In this project, you will evaluate four images at once instead of one. Please note that this document should be used alongside the Image Evaluation Guidelines, which define the criteria for Structural Integrity and Text-to-Image Alignment.
Project Guidelines

Refer to the Image Evaluation Guidelines for detailed criteria for Structural Integrity.

[New] Variety
Variety refers to the visual and conceptual differences between multiple images representing the same input prompt. The visual concept can be people, living creatures, objects and even emojis...etc. A high score is awarded when each image presents a distinct and meaningful variation in composition, pose, backdrop and additional attributes...etc. Sometimes lower variety is expected if the input prompt goes descriptive and exclusive.
Grading Scale (Variety)
Rating
Distinctiveness
Creativity
Reviewers' Perception
Low Variety
The output images show repeated concepts. There's nearly no noticible difference between them, such as slight changes in color, background details, or viewpoint which doesn't alter reviewers' experience in a meaningful way.
Or, the output images lack creativity, feeling flat and predictable. The model appears to follow a safe, literal approach to the input prompt, offering no alternative styles.
Reviewers' might find them no sense of play and surprise.
Moderate Variety
The images show some differences, but the variations are limited in scope or depth. While not identical, the outputs may show similar compositions or visual structure. Minor adjustments are present, but they do not significantly shift the reviewers' perception of the subject.
The output images show some degree of imaginative thinking, but the creative choices are only partially explored. While the images avoid being completely repetitive, they don't fully deliver surprise to the reviewers.
They don't fully deliver surprise to the reviewers.
High Variety
Each image in the set presents a clearly different visual interpretation of the subject. The variations are substantial and meaningful, such as changes in composition or visual structure. They avoid redundancy and demonstrate visually diverse response to the input prompt.
The output images go beyond straightforward interpretations of the input prompt, delivering suprises to the reviewers. These show that model is not just varying visuals, but actively visualizing user prompts from different aspects.
